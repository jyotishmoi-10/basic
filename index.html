Introduction

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, either directly or indirectly. Of the objects that orbit the Sun directly, the largest are the eight planets, with the remainder being smaller objects, such as dwarf planets and small Solar System bodies.

The Planets

  • Mercury: The smallest planet and closest to the Sun.
  • Venus: The second planet from the Sun, known for its thick atmosphere.
  • Earth: Our home planet, the third planet from the Sun.
  • Mars: The fourth planet, known as the Red Planet.
  • Jupiter: The largest planet, fifth from the Sun.
  • Saturn: The sixth planet, famous for its ring system.
  • Uranus: The seventh planet, known for its blue color and tilted axis.
  • Neptune: The eighth and farthest planet from the Sun.

Other Celestial Objects

Besides planets, the Solar System also contains dwarf planets (like Pluto), moons, comets, asteroids, and meteoroids.
